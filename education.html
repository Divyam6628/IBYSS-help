Empowering Women Through Education

Employment hurdles faced by women in rural India include lack of opportunities, gender bias and wage gaps of up to 60%. Our programmes tackle these challenges head‑on by providing free education through three institutions in rural Uttar Pradesh. These schools and colleges have won top awards for charity care and have paved the way for thousands of girls to rise above the cycle of poverty.

Building on the success of operating two schools and a college, the VSK Foundation is now supporting the initiative of establishing a vocational training institute—ensuring that young women gain practical skills for meaningful employment.

Our Educational Institutions

Kripalu Balika Primary School (KBPS)

KBPS offers quality education from Kindergarten to Class 5, laying a strong academic foundation for young learners. Children are taught core subjects—including Mathematics, English and Science—in a nurturing and joyful environment. Interactive smart boards and animated lessons help students grasp complex concepts through visual storytelling and engaging content.

Kripalu Balika Intermediate College

Serving girls from Class 6 to Class 12, this all‑girls institution has over 45 years of dedicated service in Pratapgarh. It is ranked number one in the district and seventh in the state, reflecting its longstanding commitment to academic excellence and holistic development of young women.

Kripalu Mahila Mahavidyalaya

A self‑financed women’s college offering undergraduate and postgraduate programmes, including B.Ed., B.A., B.Sc. and M.A. The college hosts the National Service Scheme (NSS), a government‑supported initiative that engages students in community service to foster social responsibility and contribute to nation‑building.
